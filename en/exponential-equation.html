Mastering Complex Exponential Equations

Learn how to solve advanced equations using power rules and algebraic identities step-by-step.

Part 1: Foundation - Essential Knowledge

1. Identities $(x \pm a)^2$

Subtraction $(-a)$:

  • $x^2 - 2x + 1 = (x - 1)^2$
  • $x^2 - 4x + 4 = (x - 2)^2$
  • $x^2 - 6x + 9 = (x - 3)^2$

Addition $(+a)$:

  • $x^2 + 2x + 1 = (x + 1)^2$
  • $x^2 + 4x + 4 = (x + 2)^2$
  • $x^2 + 6x + 9 = (x + 3)^2$

2. Exponential Rules

  • $(x^a)^b = x^{a \cdot b}$ (Ex: $(e^2)^3 = e^6$)
  • $x^a \cdot x^b = x^{a+b}$ (Ex: $e^2 \cdot e^x = e^{2+x}$)
  • $x^{-a} = \frac{1}{x^a}$ (Ex: $e^{-2} = \frac{1}{e^2}$)

Quick Challenges (Quiz)

Type 1: Polynomial Transformation

Q1.1: Transform $x^2 - 4x + 5$ into $(x-a)^2 + k$?

$x^2 - 4x + 4 + 1 = (x-2)^2 + 1$.

Q1.2: Transform $x^2 + 6x + 10$ into $(x+a)^2 + k$?

$x^2 + 6x + 9 + 1 = (x+3)^2 + 1$.

Type 2: Power Simplification

Q2.1: Simplify $(e^2)^{x^2-4x+5}$?

$e^{2(x^2-4x+5)} = e^{2(x-2)^2 + 2}$.

Q2.2: If $t = e^{2(x-2)^2}$, find $e^{4(x-2)^2}$ in terms of $t$?

$(e^{2(x-2)^2})^2 = t^2$.

Part 2: Solving the Main Problem

Solve the equation:

$$(1 + e^{-2})(e^2)^{x^2-4x+5} = e^2 + e^{4(x-2)^2}$$

Step 1: Analyzing the Exponents

Hint: Identify the identity in $x^2 - 4x + 5$.

We have: $x^2 - 4x + 5 = (x-2)^2 + 1$.
The equation becomes: $(1 + e^{-2}) \cdot e^{2[(x-2)^2 + 1]} = e^2 + e^{4(x-2)^2}$.

Step 2: Simplify and Expand

Left Hand Side (LHS): $(1 + e^{-2}) \cdot e^{2(x-2)^2} \cdot e^2 = e^2 \cdot e^{2(x-2)^2} + e^{2(x-2)^2}$.

Step 3: Substitution

Hint: Let $t = e^{2(x-2)^2}$.

Let $t = e^{2(x-2)^2} \geq 1$.
Equation: $t \cdot e^2 + t = e^2 + t^2 \Rightarrow t^2 - (e^2 + 1)t + e^2 = 0$.

Step 4: Factorization and Solving for t

$(t-1)(t-e^2) = 0 \Rightarrow t = 1$ or $t = e^2$.

Step 5: Final Solutions for x

1. $e^{2(x-2)^2} = 1 \Rightarrow x = 2$.
2. $e^{2(x-2)^2} = e^2 \Rightarrow (x-2)^2 = 1 \Rightarrow x = 1$ or $x = 3$.
**Final Solution Set: $S = \{1, 2, 3\}$.**

Part 3: Practice Challenges

Level 1: Basic

Problem 1: Solve for $x$: $2^{x^2-2x+1} = 4$

Recognize $x^2-2x+1 = (x-1)^2$ and $4 = 2^2$.
Answer: $x = 1 \pm \sqrt{2}$.
Level 2: Intermediate

Problem 2: Solve for $x$: $e^{2(x+1)^2} - (e+1)e^{(x+1)^2} + e = 0$

Substitute $t = e^{(x+1)^2}$. Solve the quadratic $t^2 - (e+1)t + e = 0$.
Answer: $x = -1, x=0, x=-2$.

🚀 Part 4: The Strategy (U-S-F)

1. Unify

Standardize complex exponents into a common form using identities.

2. Substitute

Turn exponential mess into a familiar polynomial equation for variable $t$.

3. Factorize

Group terms to find common factors. Never convert constants like $e$ to decimals too early!
