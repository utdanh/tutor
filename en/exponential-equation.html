Mastering Complex Exponential Equations

An interactive guide to mastering exponents and identities.

Part 1: Quick Warm-up

1. Identities $(x \pm a)^2$

  • $x^2 - 4x + 4 = (x - 2)^2$
  • $x^2 + 4x + 4 = (x + 2)^2$

2. Exponential Rules

  • $(a^m)^n = a^{m \cdot n}$
  • $a^m \cdot a^n = a^{m+n}$
  • $a^{-m} \cdot a^m = a^0 = 1$

Part 2: Step-by-Step Solution

$$(1 + e^{-2})(e^2)^{x^2-4x+5} = e^2 + e^{4(x-2)^2}$$

Step 1: Exponent Analysis

$x^2 - 4x + 5 = (x-2)^2 + 1$. Substitue into LHS: $(1 + e^{-2}) \cdot e^{2[(x-2)^2 + 1]}$.

Final Solutions

Set $t = e^{2(x-2)^2}$. Solutions: $t=1, t=e^2 \Rightarrow x = \{1, 2, 3\}$.

Part 3: Self-Practice Challenges

Level 1

Solve for $x$: $2^{x^2-2x+1} = 4$

Answer: $x = 1 \pm \sqrt{2}$.
Level 2

Solve: $e^{2(x+1)^2} - (e+1)e^{(x+1)^2} + e = 0$

Answer: $x = -1, 0, -2$.
Level 3

Solve: $(1 + e^{-1})e^{(x-3)^2+1} = e + e^{2(x-3)^2}$

Answer: $x=3, 2, 4$.

🚀 Part 4: The Strategy (U-S-F)

To tackle similar problems in the future, remember the "U-S-F" rule:

1. Unify

Standardize complex exponents into a common variable form (usually by using algebraic identities or isolating constants).

2. Substitute

Use a temporary variable $t$ to transform a "bulky" exponential equation into a familiar algebraic one (quadratic or cubic).

3. Factorize

Always prioritize grouping and factoring over calculating irrational numbers like $e$ or $\pi$ too early.
